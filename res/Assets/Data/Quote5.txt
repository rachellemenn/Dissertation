Quote
"<p>[I] consider these traditions both religious and cultural. Because in the Soviet Union it was difficult for my parents to uphold religious Jewish traditions, the only ones left over when they raised me were traditions I consider cultural.</p> <p>I attend seders and I had a Bar Mitzvah more because it felt culturally natural than because of of my religious beliefs.</p> <p>Fasting on Yom Kippur and lighting Shabbat candles was never really done around me, and I suppose my religious conviction isn't strong enough to make me follow traditions that were not culturally built in as a child.</p>"
